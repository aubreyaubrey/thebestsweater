For the Slow of Heart

He watched me suspiciously as I leafed through the pages, scanning titles and sayings that struck my eye. I can still rememeber the very first words I saw: “Shiva without Shakti is Shava,” a gnomic phrase that turned out to be an old Hindu saying.*

* Dr. Haridas Chaudhuri, of San Francisco’s California Institute of Asian Studies, my authority on Sanskrit and Indian philosohy, explained that shava is the Sanskrit word for corpse. Shakti is the creative, female, time-involving, world-manifesting aspect of the Supreme Being. Shiva is the God of Destruction, Redemption, and Liberation.

- Golf in the Kingdom by Michael Murphy

“True gravity, ‘tis Seamus’s term for the deeper lines o’ the force, the deeper structure of the universe. But this is the thing,” he raised his hand and shook a finger at me, “ye can only know wha’ it is by livin’ into it yersel’ – not through squeezin’ it and shovin’ it the way they do in the universities and laboratories. Ye must go into the heart o’ it, through yer own body and senses and livin’ experience, level after level right to the heart o’ it. Ye see, Michael, merely shootin’ par is second best. Goin’ for results like that leads men and cultures and entire worlds astray. But if ye do it from the inside ye get the results eventually and everythin’ else along with it.

- Golf in the Kingdom by Michael Murphy
